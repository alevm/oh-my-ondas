# Oh My Ondas — Statement of Intent

Oh My Ondas is a portable instrument for pedestrian urban exploration.
It creates location-authenticated audio compositions by binding sound to
specific place-time coordinates — sonic postcards that could only exist
at that exact intersection of geography, moment, and environment.

## What this is for

This instrument is designed for slowness and attention. It is for people
who walk through cities and want to listen differently. It is for field
recordists, experimental musicians, situationist practitioners, and anyone
who believes that a specific place deserves a specific sound.

The pedestrian scale is not incidental — it is the point. The device is
calibrated for the speed of walking, the duration of wandering, the
intimacy of being present in a place without the mediation of a vehicle
or screen.

## What this is not for

This instrument is not appropriate for:

- Automotive or in-vehicle applications
- Productivity, efficiency, or workflow optimization contexts  
- Surveillance or location monitoring
- Commercial experiences that extract value from place without returning
  attention to it
- Any application that requires the user to be moving faster than walking

These exclusions are not legal clauses. They are a clear statement of
intention, made so that anyone who builds on this work understands what
they are building on and can make an honest decision about whether their
use is aligned with it.

## On copying and building

The hardware and firmware are open. Build your own. Modify it. Learn from it.
Port it to different microcontrollers. Write alternative firmware.
Create derivative instruments.

If you do, please credit the origin, keep your modifications open under
the same terms, and carry the pedestrian philosophy forward or be explicit
that you are departing from it.

If you are a commercial entity considering using this work, the AGPL license
requires you to release your modifications. Beyond the legal requirement:
please consider whether your use is consistent with what this instrument is.
