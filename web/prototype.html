Quick Start

  1. Solder headers onto Teensy 4.1 and Audio Shield. Stack the Audio Shield on top of the Teensy (pins align). Solder headers onto the ESP32 DevKit.
  2. Place boards on breadboards. Teensy + Audio Shield on the left breadboard, ESP32 on the right. Connect the 5V and GND rails between both boards with jumper wires.
  3. Wire the I2C bus. Connect Teensy pin 18 (SDA) and pin 19 (SCL) to the OLED display and MPR121. Add 4.7K pull-up resistors on both lines to 3.3V.
  4. Wire the encoders. Each encoder has 3 connections (CLK, DT, SW) plus GND. Use 10K pull-up resistors on each signal line, or enable internal pull-ups in firmware.
  5. Wire the buttons. Connect MODE (pin 26), SHIFT (pin 27), REC (pin 28), PLAY (pin 29) to GND through tactile buttons. Internal pull-ups are enabled in firmware.
  6. Wire the LED ring. Connect WS2812B data-in to Teensy pin 30. Power the ring from the 5V rail. Add a 300-470Ω resistor on the data line and a 100µF capacitor across the 5V/GND supply.
  7. Wire the 3.5mm jacks. Connect Line In and Headphone Out to the Audio Shield's built-in jack headers. If using external jacks, wire tip/ring/sleeve to the corresponding Audio Shield pins.
  8. Connect ESP32. Wire Teensy pin 17 (TX) to ESP32 RX, and Teensy pin 16 (RX) to ESP32 TX. Share 3.3V and GND. This provides WiFi/BT for GPS and location services.
  9. Prepare the SD card. Format a microSD card as FAT32. Create directories: /samples/, /patterns/, /recordings/, /presets/. Copy WAV samples (44.1kHz, 16-bit) into /samples/.
  10. Flash the firmware. Install PlatformIO, then run: pio run -e teensy41 -t upload from the firmware/teensy/ directory. The Teensy Loader will flash automatically over USB.
  11. Power on and test. Connect headphones to the output jack. The OLED should show the boot screen. Tap pads to trigger samples, turn encoders to adjust mix and effects. Press PLAY to start the sequencer.
