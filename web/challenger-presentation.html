2.1 Core Messaging

One-Liner

Oh My Ondas is a sonic camera - it photographs the soundscape of a place and turns it into music you can remember and interact with.

30-Second Pitch

30 seconds
Oh My Ondas is a sonic camera for places. It captures the sounds of a location - ambient noise, radio, environmental audio - and transforms them into music that remembers and represents that specific place. You can listen back and recall the place, or interact with it live. We have a working web demo you can try right now, and we're looking for partners in museums, media, and events.

2-Minute Pitch

2 minutes
Thank you for meeting. I'm [NAME], creator of Oh My Ondas. THE PROBLEM: Places have unique sounds, but there's no easy way to capture that sonic identity and turn it into something you can keep, share, or build on. Museums, events, and media producers want audio experiences that truly come from a place - not generic stock music. OUR SOLUTION: Oh My Ondas is a sonic camera. It photographs the soundscape of any location - ambient noise, radio signals, environmental audio - and transforms those sounds into music that remembers and represents that place. HOW IT WORKS: We combine 8 audio sources - microphones, radio, sensors - with a professional sequencer and AI that responds to what it hears. The music is derived from the place itself. GPS and timestamp give context. WORKING NOW: Unlike concepts, you can try Oh My Ondas right now at alevm.github.io/oh-my-ondas. Full 8-track sequencer, synthesizer, effects, AI patterns - all working. THE ASK: We're looking for pilot partners - museums wanting sonic experiences derived from their spaces, media producers who want music that sounds like the places they film, or event organizers creating unrepeatable sonic moments. Can I show you a quick demo?

2.3 Three Operational Modes

📷

PICTURE MODE

What: 90-second environmental capture, unprocessed

Use: Documentation, archival, raw soundscapes

Output: Audio file with GPS/timestamp metadata

🎨

SOUNDSCAPE MODE

What: AI analyzes location and creates composition

Use: Ambient installations, sonic postcards

Output: AI-generated piece reflecting place character

🎹

INTERACT MODE

What: Live performance starting from captured base

Use: Performances, workshops, audience participation

Output: Collaborative composition with all contributions

2.5 Use Cases by Challenger Type

FOR MUSEUMS (Moco, CaixaForum+)
"Sonic Souvenirs" - Each gallery room sounds different. Capture that.
  1. Visitor enters room with phone/device
  2. Oh My Ondas photographs the room's soundscape
  3. AI transforms those sounds into a musical composition
  4. Visitor takes home music that sounds like that room
  5. Listening back recalls the place - a sonic memory

VALUE:

  • Unique visitor experience
  • Shareable content (social proof)
  • Extended engagement post-visit
  • Data on visitor flow
  • Gift shop revenue (premium exports)
FOR BROADCAST/MEDIA (Gestmusic, Lavinia)
"Sound of the Story" - Music derived from the actual location you're filming.
  1. Capture the soundscape at the filming location
  2. Transform location sounds into production-ready music
  3. Audio beds and compositions that carry the feel of the place
  4. GPS/timestamp metadata for production context

VALUE:

  • Music that sounds like the location
  • Authentic audio identity per production
  • AI-assisted real-time composition
  • Multi-source flexibility
  • Competitive differentiation
FOR EVENTS (Festivals, Corporate)
"Crowd Sonic Portraits" - The event's sounds become its music.
  1. Capture the sounds of the crowd, venue, performances
  2. Transform those sounds into a musical composition
  3. AI creates music that sounds like THIS event
  4. Attendees take home music made from the sounds they were part of
  5. Different each event - unrepeatable by nature

VALUE:

  • Memorable takeaway
  • Social sharing driver
  • Brand differentiation
  • Engagement metric
  • Revenue opportunity

2.7 FAQ / Objection Handling

"Is this a real company?"
"I'm an independent developer based in Barcelona. For a pilot, I can invoice as freelance or set up a company quickly if needed. The technology is real and working - that's what matters for a proof of concept."
"The hardware isn't ready?"
"The web version has full functionality - that's actually an advantage. No installation, works on any device, easier to deploy. Hardware version adds portability but isn't required for a pilot."
"Why should we work with a solo developer?"
"You get direct access to the creator, fast iterations, and flexible terms. No corporate bureaucracy. For a pilot, that's ideal. If we scale, I'll build a team."
"What about IP/ownership?"
"The core technology is mine. You'd get a license for your use case. Any custom features we develop together, we can discuss ownership - I'm open to fair arrangements."
"This seems niche..."
"The more AI-generated generic content there is, the more valuable authentic place-based experiences become. Music made from real sounds of real places is inherently unique. We're early, which means partnership opportunity."
